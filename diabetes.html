Diabetes

what is the diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs when the pancreas does not produce enough insulin
When the body is unable to effectively use the insulin it produces

symptoms

Feeling very thirsty
An increased need to urinate than usual
Blurred vision
feeling tired
Unintentional weight loss.
Diabetes can cause permanent vision loss due to damage to the blood vessels in the eyes.

Methods of prevention

Achieving and maintaining a healthy body weight

Maintain physical activity by doing moderate activity for at least 30 minutes a day

Follow a healthy diet while limiting sugary substances and saturated fats

Non-smoking


Tips for living with diabetes

Monitoring the effect of medications and insulin on glucose level

Clean hands with soap and water to avoid food residue entering the test device.

Control the needle diameter and depth according to the patient's comfort.

Most test kits only need one drop of blood.

Taking a blood sample from the tip of the finger to reduce pain and ensure an accurate result.

Close the needle cap after completion to avoid injuries.
