LIVER

what is the the Liver

The liver is an organ the size of a football.
It is located just below the rib cage on the right side.
The liver is necessary to digest food and rid the body of toxic substances.
Liver diseases can be inherited (hereditary)


Liver functions

1. Storage of sugars and fats
2. Producing proteins that help blood clot and prevent bleeding
3. Production of bile juice that digests fats
4. Elimination of harmful substances, such as alcohol and drugs, to be excreted through urine and feces.


The causes of liver cancer

1 cirrhosis of the liver
2. Long-term infection with hepatitis B or C virus
3. Drinking large amounts of alcohol over a long period of time
4. Type 2 diabetes and smoking
5. Family medical history Limit family members (father, mother, sister or brother)
6. Toxins such as aflatoxin, found in moldy peanuts and grains.


symptoms

1 Loss of appetite and feeling full very quickly after eating.
2. Weight loss for no apparent reason.
3. Patients feeling (nausea) and vomiting.
4. Pain in the liver area and a high fever
5. Flatulence and swelling caused by fluid buildup (called ascites).
6. Yellowing of the skin and the whites of the eyes


Prevention of liver cancer:

1 Vaccination against hepatitis B virus (HBV).
2. Not using the patient's tools with hepatitis virus infection, such as razors, toothbrushes, and towels.
3. Not to be exposed to other body secretions from a person infected with the hepatitis virus, such as blood and saliva.
4. When infected with the hepatitis virus, it is best to seek treatment before developing cirrhosis.
5. In case of cirrhosis of the liver, the doctor must be followed up periodically to detect early liver tumor
